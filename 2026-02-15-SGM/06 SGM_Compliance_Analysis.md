**Risk:** An owner who wishes to challenge the SGM on procedural grounds could rely on this defect. Its materiality depends on whether any owner can demonstrate they were prejudiced by the 2-day shortfall.

## B. Was the Postponement Procedurally Correct?

### B1. The "30-Minute Adjournment" — Legal Precision

**The minutes state:**
> *"The President wished to provide more time for maximum participation by Owners for the Special General Meeting at 3pm and hence the meeting was adjourned by 30 mins."*

**Bye-Laws Clause 16 — Adjourned Meetings:**
> *"If the meeting of owners cannot be organised because a quorum has not attended, the owners who are present, may adjourn the meeting to a time not less than forty-eight hours from the time the original meeting was called."*

**Analysis:**

Clause 16 defines an "adjournment" very specifically — it is a mechanism triggered *only when quorum has not attended*, and the rescheduled meeting must be **at least 48 hours later**.

The 30-minute delay described in the minutes is not a Clause 16 adjournment. It is an informal delay before the meeting was formally convened. However, the minutes use the word "adjourned" — a legally precise term with a defined meaning in the Bye-Laws.

**Finding:** The use of the word "adjourned" for a 30-minute wait is **legally imprecise and constitutes a procedural irregularity**. It conflates two different concepts:

1. An informal delay before the meeting starts (no Bye-Law provision)
2. A formal adjournment due to lack of quorum (Clause 16 — requires 48-hour minimum)

If challenged, an owner could argue that the meeting was improperly "adjourned" at 3pm and then "reconvened" without compliance with the 48-hour rule. The correct language to use would have been that the start of the meeting was *delayed* by 30 minutes.

---

### B2. ⚠️ Critical Finding: Quorum Was NOT Met

**The minutes claim:**
> *"Post-adjournment, the President noted that while the Quorum for a Special General Meeting was present..."*

**Quorum under Bye-Laws Clause 9:**
> *"Except as otherwise provided in these Bye-laws, the presence in person of a majority of owners shall constitute a Quorum."*

**Clause 2.6 — Definition of Majority:**
> *"'MAJORITY OF OWNERS' means those owners holding 51% of the votes in accordance with the percentages assigned in the Declaration."*

| Calculation | Value |
|---|---|
| Total apartments / owners | 264 |
| Majority quorum required | 51% × 264 = **minimum 135 owners** |
| Owners present (from attendance register) | **25** |
| Attendance as % of total | 9.5% |
| **Primary quorum met?** | ❌ **NO — 25 << 135** |

The attendance register appended to the minutes records **25 signatories**. If this register is complete, then not only was primary quorum not present — even the *adjourned meeting* threshold of 20% (Bye-Laws Clause 16) was not met:

| Calculation | Value |
|---|---|
| Adjourned meeting quorum required | 20% × 264 = **minimum 53 owners** |
| Owners present (from attendance register) | **25** |
| **Adjourned meeting quorum met?** | ❌ **NO — 25 < 53** |

**This is the most significant finding in this analysis.**

The minutes assert that quorum was present, but the attendance register in the same document records only 25 attendees — well below the 135 needed for primary quorum and even below the 53 needed for an adjourned meeting.

**Possible explanations** (none confirmed by the documents):

1. The attendance register is *incomplete* — additional owners were present but did not sign.
2. The meeting was treated as an adjourned meeting on the basis that the primary meeting at 3pm lacked quorum, and the 30-minute delay was the "adjournment" — but this would violate Clause 16's 48-hour minimum gap.
3. The "majority of owners" was being interpreted as majority of those *present*, not majority of all 264 owners — but this contradicts Clause 2.6's explicit definition.

**Risk:** If the attendance register is the complete record, all five resolutions passed at this meeting may be voidable on the ground that quorum was never validly constituted. Any owner could challenge the resolutions on this basis.

**Recommendation:** The Association should verify and document the full attendance at the meeting. If additional owners were present but did not sign, a supplementary attendance record should be created and ratified. If 25 was the actual attendance, the resolutions should be treated as indicative/in-principle only and the formal consent collection drive should be the binding process.

### B3. The 75% Threshold for DoD Amendment

The minutes acknowledge:
> *"...the specific statutory quorum (75%) required for finalizing the Amendment to the Deed of Declaration was not physically present."*

This 75% threshold appears to come from **Paragraph 18 of the Deed of Declaration** (referenced in Resolutions 2 and 3 of the minutes). It is *higher* than the Bye-Laws' own 2/3rds threshold for Bye-Law amendments (Clause 53).

| Amendment type | Threshold | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Bye-Laws amendment | 2/3rds of all apartments = **177 owners** | Bye-Laws Clause 53 |
| DoD amendment | 75% of all apartments = **199 owners** | DoD Paragraph 18 |
| Owners present (attendance register) | 25 | SGM Register |

Neither threshold was met. The minutes correctly acknowledge this and authorized a consent collection drive — which is an appropriate response, provided the consent collection process is itself legally valid (individual written consents or PoA-backed signatures).

### C2. Agenda Item 4 — KAOA Section Reference Discrepancy

**Notice (item 4):**
> *"...Deeds of Apartment in accordance with Sections 9(2), 12 and 14 of the KAOA, 1972..."*

**Resolution 4 (minutes):**
> *"...Sections 5(2) and 12 of the Karnataka Apartment Ownership Act, 1972..."*

The KAOA sections cited are **different**:

| Document | Sections cited |
|---|---|
| Notice | Sections 9(2), 12 and 14 |
| Resolution 4 (Minutes) | Sections 5(2) and 12 |

This is not merely a drafting variation — Section 9(2) and Section 5(2) are different provisions of the Act. Section 5(2) relates to the Declaration to be executed by apartment owners, while Section 9(2) relates to the Deed of Apartment.

**Finding:** This is a **factual discrepancy** between the notice and the resolution as passed. The resolution voted on differed in its legal basis from what was noticed. While the subject matter (Deeds of Apartment) is the same, the statutory authority cited changed between the notice and the resolution.

### C3. ⚠️ Agenda Item 5 — NOC-Linked Compliance Clause Not Addressed

**Notice (item 5):**
> *"To approve and incorporate a mandatory 'NOC-linked compliance clause' in the Deed of Apartment, stipulating that execution and registration of the Deed of Apartment shall be a mandatory pre-condition for issuance of any No-Objection Certificate (NOC) by the Board of Office Bearers for any future sale, transfer or conveyance of an apartment."*

The minutes contain **no standalone resolution** on this item. The NOC-linked compliance clause is mentioned in the minutes only within the introductory summary of agenda items, but never in any of the five passed resolutions.

**Bye-Laws Clause 14:**
> *"No other business shall be transacted at a Special Meeting, except as stated in the notice, without the consent of four-fifths of the Owners present in person."*

The inverse is also true: if a matter is stated in the notice, it ought to be addressed. Omitting a noticed item without recording a decision (even a decision to defer) creates ambiguity about whether this was voted on, rejected, or simply forgotten.

**Finding:** Agenda item 5 (NOC-linked compliance clause) was **not addressed in the resolutions**. This is a gap in the minutes. The community should clarify whether this item was:
- Voted on and approved (in which case it should be added to the minutes)
- Voted on and rejected (in which case that should be recorded)
- Deferred to a future meeting (in which case that should be recorded)

### C4. ⚠️ Non-Agenda Business — Board Continuation Decision

The minutes record the following decision at the end of the meeting, which was **not in the notice**:

> *"...the existing Board of Managers, by whatever name called, and office bearers shall continue in office and be deemed duly authorized to exercise all powers and perform all acts required in that regard."*

This is a significant governance decision — authorizing the existing Board to continue without fresh elections — which was transacted at the SGM without being in the published notice.

**Bye-Laws Clause 14:**
> *"No other business shall be transacted at a Special Meeting, except as stated in the notice, without the consent of four-fifths of the Owners present in person."*

For this non-agenda item to have been validly transacted, **four-fifths (80%) of the owners present in person** must have consented. The minutes do not record any such specific consent being sought or given for this item as a non-agenda matter.

| Calculation | Value |
|---|---|
| Owners present (from register) | 25 |
| 80% of 25 | 20 owners must consent |
| Consent for non-agenda item recorded? | ❌ Not mentioned in minutes |

**Finding:** The Board continuation decision is **non-agenda business transacted without the procedural step required by Clause 14** (four-fifths consent). Even if 80% of those present did consent, this is not recorded. Combined with the quorum issue (Section B2), the legal validity of this decision is doubtful.

## E. Recommendations

**Immediate actions:**

1. **Verify and document full attendance.** If additional owners were present but did not sign, obtain a sworn/affirmed statement from the President/Secretary and append it to the minutes record. If 25 was indeed the full attendance, formally acknowledge that primary and adjourned quorum were not met.

2. **Treat all resolutions as "in-principle approvals" only**, as acknowledged in the minutes, pending the formal consent collection drive.

3. **Ensure the consent drive is legally robust.** Written individual consents should identify the specific resolution being approved, reference the meeting date, and be verifiable. Electronic consents should meet applicable e-signature standards.

4. **Address the NOC-linked compliance clause** (Notice item 5) explicitly — either pass a resolution on it or record a decision to defer, so it does not fall into a legal vacuum.

5. **Address the Board continuation decision** at the next meeting — either ratify it through a properly noticed resolution, or hold elections.

6. **Avoid using the word "adjourned" for informal delays** in future minutes — use "the start of the meeting was delayed by 30 minutes."

7. **For future SGMs:** Post notice on the notice board at least 10 days before the meeting (Clause 15.2), not just on the day the postal notice is sent.
