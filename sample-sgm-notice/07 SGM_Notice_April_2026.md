### AGENDA ITEM 1: Amendment of Bye-Law Clause 2.3 — Correcting the Definition of "Board"

**Purpose:** To amend **Clause 2.3** of the Bye-Laws to redefine the Board of Managers as an elected body of 10 Apartment Owners, replacing the current erroneous definition that equates the Board with all 264 owners.

**The current text of Clause 2.3 reads:**

> *"'BOARD' means a Board of Managers consisting of 264 persons, all of whom shall be owners of the Apartments in the 'SOBHA JASMINE' Building."*

**The proposed amended text of Clause 2.3 shall read:**

> *"'BOARD' means a Board of Managers consisting of 10 persons elected by the Apartment Owners from among themselves, in accordance with these Bye-Laws."*

**Why this amendment is necessary:**

The current Bye-Laws contain an error in **Clause 2.3 (Definition of "Board")**, which defines the Board of Managers as consisting of all 264 Apartment Owners. This definition renders the Board operationally identical to the General Body and makes structured governance unworkable — there is no distinct, accountable executive body responsible for day-to-day administration.

This is inconsistent with the intent of the Bye-Laws themselves, which elsewhere contemplate the Board as a smaller, elected body. For example, **Clause 13 (Annual Meeting)** states that *"there shall be elected by ballot of the Apartment Owners, Board of Office Bearers"* — implying the Board is elected from among the owners, not identical to all owners. **Clause 22 (Vacancies)** refers to *"vacancies in the Board of Managers"* being filled by *"vote of the majority of the remaining Managers"* — a provision that is meaningless if all 264 owners are already on the Board. **Clause 23 (Removal of Manager)** provides for removal of individual Managers by a majority vote of owners — again, a provision that only makes sense if the Board is a subset of owners. **Clause 30 (Designation)** states that the President, Secretary, and Treasurer *"shall be elected by, and from the Board of Managers"* — presupposing a distinct, smaller body. And **Clause 31 (Election of Office Bearers)** states that *"the Board of Managers shall be elected at the end of every 2 years"* — confirming the Board is an elected body, not the entire ownership.

This amendment corrects that inconsistency and brings Clause 2.3 in line with the rest of the Bye-Laws.

**Voting threshold for this amendment:** In accordance with **Clause 53 (Amendment of Bye-Laws)**, this amendment requires the approval of owners representing **at least two-thirds (2/3rds) of the total number of all Apartments** in the building as shown in the Declaration. This is a higher threshold than a simple majority and reflects the importance of bye-law amendments.

> Clause 53 states: *"These Bye-laws may be amended by the Association in a duly constituted meeting for such purpose, and no amendment shall take effect unless approved by owners representing at least 2/3rd of the total number of all Apartments in the Building as shown in the Declaration."*

### AGENDA ITEM 2: Election of a Board of Managers Comprising 10 Members

**Purpose:** Following the amendment of Clause 2.3 under Agenda Item 1, to elect by ballot a Board of Managers consisting of 10 Apartment Owners who will manage the affairs of the Association and govern the day-to-day functioning of the condominium.

**What will happen at the SGM:**

Nominations for the Board of Managers will be accepted from eligible Apartment Owners. Eligibility is governed by **Clause 7 (Disqualification)**, which provides that no owner in arrears of common expenses for more than 60 days shall be entitled to stand for election. If more than 10 nominations are received, an election shall be conducted by ballot, as provided for in **Clause 13 (Annual Meeting)**. The 10 elected members shall constitute the Board of Managers and shall hold office for a term of 2 years, as stated in **Clause 31 (Election of Office Bearers)**. The elected Board shall, from among themselves, appoint a President, Secretary, and Treasurer, as required by **Clauses 30 (Designation) and 31**.

**Nomination process:** A separate communication containing the nomination form and timeline for filing nominations will be sent by the Secretary by end of day Sunday, 29 March 2026.

**Note:** This agenda item is conditional on the successful passage of Agenda Item 1. If the amendment under Agenda Item 1 does not receive the requisite two-thirds majority, this item will not be taken up, and the meeting will be adjourned for the Board to determine the next steps.

## QUORUM REQUIREMENTS

Apartment Owners are reminded of the quorum rules that govern this meeting.

**First convening (5 April 2026 at 3:00 PM):** As per **Clause 9 (Quorum)**, the presence in person of a **majority of owners (i.e., owners holding 51% of the votes)** is required to constitute a quorum. Per **Clause 2.6**, "Majority of Owners" means those owners holding 51% of the votes in accordance with the percentages assigned in the Declaration.

**If quorum is not met — Adjournment:** As per **Clause 16 (Adjourned Meetings)**, if quorum is not present, the owners who are present may adjourn the meeting to a date and time **not less than 48 hours** from the time the original meeting was called. A fresh notice of the adjourned meeting will be communicated.

**At the adjourned meeting:** If quorum is again not present at the adjourned meeting, then as per **Clause 16**, owners present in person, being **not less than 20% of the total ownership**, shall form a quorum, and the meeting may proceed.

**Important note on Agenda Item 1:** Even if the meeting proceeds with a reduced quorum under Clause 16, the bye-law amendment under Agenda Item 1 will still require approval from owners representing at least **two-thirds (2/3rds)** of all apartments, as mandated by **Clause 53**. The reduced quorum provision does not reduce this voting threshold.

All Apartment Owners of Sobha Jasmine Condominium, as defined in **Clause 2.7** of the Bye-Laws, are eligible to attend this meeting.
