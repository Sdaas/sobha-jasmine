# Governance Context: KAOA 1972 vs KSRA 1960
## Which Law Governs Sobha Jasmine Condominium?

---

## 1. The Short Answer

**Sobha Jasmine Condominium is governed exclusively by the Karnataka Apartment Ownership Act, 1972 (KAOA).** It is not, and has never been, governed by the Karnataka Societies Registration Act, 1960 (KSRA).

The KSRA-registered entity referenced in the SGM documents is a separate, parallel *welfare assoc iation* — an informal body that historically managed day-to-day operations. It has no statutory authority over the condominium's common areas, property rights, or governance. The entire purpose of the February 2026 SGM was, in part, to wind down that KSRA entity and consolidate governance under KAOA.

---

## 2. Proof from the Documents

The Bye-Laws of Sobha Jasmine Condominium (Exhibit B, `01 Exhibit_B_Bye_Laws.md`) establish KAOA as the governing framework in four separate, unambiguous clauses:

**Clause 2.1 — Definition of "Act":**
> *"'ACT' means the Karnataka Apartment Ownership Act, 1972."*

**Clause 3 — Apartment Ownership:**
> *"The building located at Survey No's. 31/2, 31/3 and 31/4... and known as 'SOBHA JASMINE' is submitted to the provisions of the Karnataka Apartment Ownership Act, 1972."*

**Clause 5.1 — Members of Association:**
> *"All persons who have purchased constructed Apartments... shall execute respective Declarations under Section 5 (ii) of the Karnataka Apartment Ownership Act, 1972, submitting their Apartments to the provisions of the Act."*

**Clause 51 — Compliance (overriding provision):**
> *"These Bye-laws are set forth to comply with the requirements of the Karnataka Apartment Ownership Act, 1972. In case, any of these Bye-laws conflict with the provision of the said Act, it is hereby agreed and accepted that the provisions of the Act will apply."*

The Bye-Laws themselves are framed *"As per Performa given in Exhibit-'B' of FORM-'A' prescribed by Rule 3 of the Karnataka Apartment Ownership Rules, 1974"* — the model form prescribed under the KAOA framework.

The SGM Minutes (`04 SGM_15_Feb_2026_Minutes.md`) additionally record Resolution 1 as:
> *"...the Association of Apartment Owners of 'Sobha Jasmine Condominium' do hereby formally reaffirm... that the apartment owners and the Association... stand governed exclusively by the provisions of the Karnataka Apartment Ownership Act, 1972..."*

---

## 3. What is KAOA 1972?

The **Karnataka Apartment Ownership Act, 1972** (Karnataka Act No. 17 of 1973, in force from 1 April 1975) is a *dedicated* statute for apartment ownership in Karnataka. It provides a comprehensive legal framework for:

- Making individual apartments heritable and transferable property with a separate Khata
- Defining each owner's undivided share (UDS) in common areas
- Creating a mandatory Association of Apartment Owners with statutory powers
- Prescribing governance through a Board of Managers (not a committee)
- Governing meetings, quorum, bye-laws, accounts and disputes

**Key structural features of KAOA:**

| Feature | KAOA 1972 |
|---|---|
| Registration authority | Sub-Registrar (through Declaration) |
| Governing document | Deed of Declaration + Bye-Laws (Exhibit B) |
| Governance body | Board of Managers (statutory) |
| Membership | All apartment owners — mandatory |
| Property rights | Each apartment is independently heritable and transferable |
| Common areas | UDS (Undivided Share) assigned to each apartment in Declaration |
| Legal standing | Association can hold property, enter contracts, sue and be sued |
| Quorum (general rule) | Majority of owners (51%) in person — Section 16 / Bye-Laws Clause 9 |
| Adjourned meeting quorum | Not less than 20% (Sobha Jasmine Bye-Laws Clause 16) |
| Notice for meetings | Minimum 7 days, maximum 15 days (Bye-Laws Clause 15.1) |
| Notice board posting | Minimum 10 days (Bye-Laws Clause 15.2) |
| Amendment of Bye-Laws | Minimum 2/3rds of all apartment owners (Bye-Laws Clause 53) |
| Amendment of DoD | 75% of owners as per Para 18 of Sobha Jasmine DoD |

**Key KAOA sections relevant to meetings (Section 16):**

Section 16 of KAOA mandates that every condominium's bye-laws shall provide for:
- *(b)* Method of calling meetings; what percentage (if other than majority) constitutes quorum
- *(d)* Maintenance, repair and replacement of common areas
- *(k)* Amendment procedures for bye-laws

Section 16 further provides that: *"No modification of or amendment to the bye-law shall be valid, unless set forth in an amendment to the Declaration... and a copy thereof is duly filed with the competent authority."*

## 4. What is KSRA 1960?

The **Karnataka Societies Registration Act, 1960** (Karnataka Act No. 17 of 1960) is a *general* statute originally designed for literary, scientific, charitable, and educational societies. It was not designed for apartment governance.

**Key structural features of KSRA:**

| Feature | KSRA 1960 |
|---|---|
| Registration authority | Registrar of Societies |
| Governing document | Memorandum of Association + Rules and Regulations |
| Governance body | Governing Body / Committee (as defined in Rules) |
| Membership | Voluntary — minimum 7 members to register |
| Property rights | Property vested in the Governing Body, not individual members |
| Common areas | No UDS concept — no statutory allocation |
| Legal standing | Can sue and be sued in name of Secretary |
| Quorum | Depends on Rules (not standardised by the Act) |
| Notice for SGM (dissolution) | **21 days** prior to the Special General Meeting (Section 22) |
| Notice for AGM | As per society's own rules |
| Dissolution | 3/4 of members must vote for dissolution, confirmed at a second SGM after 30 days (Section 22) |
| Amendment of Rules | As per each society's own rules |

**KSRA Section 22 — Dissolution (exact provision):**

*"Any number not less than three-fourths of the members of any society may determine that it shall be dissolved... No such proposition shall be deemed to have been approved unless such report shall have been delivered or sent by post to every member of the society, twenty-one days previous to the date of the special general meeting convened by the governing body for the consideration thereof."*

*"...confirmed by a similar majority of votes at a second special general meeting convened by the governing body after an interval of thirty days after the former meeting."*

## 5. Key Differences: KAOA vs KSRA

| Parameter | KAOA 1972 | KSRA 1960 |
|---|---|---|
| **Designed for** | Apartment ownership | Literary/charitable/educational societies |
| **Membership** | All apartment owners (compulsory) | Minimum 7, voluntary |
| **Property ownership** | Each owner holds independent title + UDS | Property vested in Governing Body |
| **Legal authority to manage** | Full statutory authority over common areas | Courts have held KSRA societies "cannot carry out maintenance activity" for apartments |
| **Governance body** | Board of Managers (defined in Bye-Laws, mandatory) | Governing Body/Committee (defined in Rules) |
| **Meetings — notice for SGM** | 7–15 days (Sobha Jasmine Bye-Laws Cl. 15.1) | **21 days** (Section 22, for dissolution SGMs) |
| **Meetings — notice board** | 10 days (Sobha Jasmine Bye-Laws Cl. 15.2) | Not specified in Act |
| **Quorum** | Majority of owners (51%) | Depends on society's own rules |
| **Adjourned meeting quorum** | 20% (Sobha Jasmine Bye-Laws Cl. 16) | As per rules |
| **Bye-Law/Rule amendment** | 2/3rds of all owners (Bye-Laws Cl. 53) | As per society's rules |
| **DoD amendment** | 75% of owners (Sobha Jasmine DoD Para 18) | N/A |
| **Dissolution** | N/A (owners hold property independently) | 3/4 members, two SGMs 30 days apart |
| **Enforcement of maintenance** | Legally enforceable | Legally challenged |
| **Separate Khata** | Yes — each apartment has independent Khata | No |

## 6. Why Many Apartments Registered Under KSRA (and Why It Is Problematic)

When Sobha Jasmine was built and occupied, the KAOA framework — while legally correct — was operationally complex. Developers and residents found it easier to register a simple welfare association under KSRA (requiring only 7 members and a simple registration with the Registrar of Societies) to handle day-to-day maintenance.

This created a *dual-governance* situation: a legally valid KAOA condominium with a legally invalid (for this purpose) KSRA welfare association running operations in parallel.

Courts in Karnataka have repeatedly held that KSRA-registered welfare associations *"cannot carry out maintenance activity"* and are considered *"illegal bodies"* for the purpose of apartment governance. The Association of Apartment Owners under KAOA is the only body with lawful authority.

This is precisely why Resolution 5 of the February 2026 SGM authorizes the KAOA Association to receive funds, assets and records from the KSRA welfare association — as a step toward consolidating lawful governance under KAOA.

---

## 7. Summary

Sobha Jasmine is unambiguously a KAOA condominium. The Bye-Laws, the Deed of Declaration, and four explicit clauses in the Bye-Laws confirm this. The KSRA welfare association is a separate, parallel entity without statutory authority over the condominium, which is in the process of being wound down through a separate KSRA SGM. For all governance matters — meetings, notice, quorum, elections, bye-law amendments — the Sobha Jasmine Bye-Laws (which are based on the KAOA model) are the primary reference, with KAOA overriding in case of conflict (Clause 51).
