# Special General Meeting (SGM) Procedures — Sobha Jasmine Condominium

## A Detailed Reference Guide Based on the Bye-Laws (Exhibit B)

**Source Document:** Exhibit B — Bye-Laws of "SOBHA JASMINE CONDOMINIUM", framed under the Karnataka Apartment Ownership Act, 1972 and Rules, 1974.

**Audience:** Apartment Owners and Association Committee Members

**Scope:** This report is based exclusively on the text of the Bye-Laws. Where the Bye-Laws are silent on a matter, this is explicitly noted as a **gap**.

---

## 1. Who Can Call a Special General Meeting (SGM)?

The Bye-Laws identify **two routes** through which an SGM can be called:

**Clause 14 — Special Meetings** states:

> *"It shall be the duty of the President to call a Special Meeting of the Apartment Owners, as directed by a resolution of the Board, or upon a petition signed by a majority of the Owners and having been presented to the Secretary, or any person authorised under the Act, in this regard."*

This means an SGM can be triggered by:

1. **The Board of Office Bearers** — by passing a resolution directing the President to call the meeting.
2. **The Apartment Owners** — by submitting a petition signed by a **majority of the Owners** (i.e., owners holding 51% of votes, per Clause 2.6) to the Secretary.

In both cases, it is the **President** who bears the formal duty to call the meeting. The Secretary's role is to receive petitions and issue notices (see Section 2 below).

**Note:** The Bye-Laws also reference "any person authorised under the Act" — this refers to the Karnataka Apartment Ownership Act, 1972, which may provide additional mechanisms for convening meetings.

---

## 2. What Is the Process for Calling the Meeting? What Is the Notice Period?

The notice process is governed by **Clause 15 — Notice of Meetings**:

**Clause 15.1** states:

> *"It shall be the duty of the Secretary to mail or send a notice of each Annual or Special Meeting, stating the purpose thereof, as well as the place and the time it is to be held, to each Apartment Owner, at least 7 days but not more than 15 days prior to such meeting. The mailing or sending of a notice in the manner provided in this Bye-laws shall be considered as notice served."*

**Clause 15.2** adds:

> *"The said notices will be either by ordinary post or by hand delivery to each of the Apartment. Apart from the dispatch stated above, the said Notices will also be put up on the notice board at least 10 days before the meeting is to be held."*

**Summary of notice requirements:**

- **Who sends it:** The Secretary.
- **Minimum notice period:** 7 days before the meeting.
- **Maximum notice period:** 15 days before the meeting.
- **Methods of delivery:** Ordinary post or hand delivery to each apartment.
- **Additional requirement:** Notice must also be displayed on the notice board at least 10 days before the meeting.
- **Content of notice:** Must state the purpose, place, and time of the meeting.

**Clause 14** further requires that:

> *"The notice of any Special Meeting shall state the place, the time of such meeting and the purpose thereof."*

**Clause 12 — Place of Meetings** specifies:

> *"Meetings of the Association shall be held at a suitable place, convenient to the owners, in the Building."*

---

## 3. Can the SGM Be "Open-Ended"? Or Must It Have a Specific Agenda?

The Bye-Laws are clear: an **SGM must have a specific agenda**.

**Clause 14 — Special Meetings** states:

> *"The notice of any Special Meeting shall state the place, the time of such meeting and the purpose thereof."*

The word **"purpose"** here means that the notice must specify the agenda items to be discussed. This is reinforced by the restriction on non-agenda business (see Section 8 below), which would be meaningless if meetings could be open-ended.

**Conclusion:** An SGM cannot be open-ended. The notice must clearly state the specific purpose(s) for which the meeting is being convened.

---

## 4. Who Is Eligible to Attend the SGM?

The Bye-Laws do not have a dedicated clause restricting SGM attendance. However, eligibility is governed by several provisions read together:

**Clause 2.7** defines who is an Owner:

> *"'OWNER' or 'APARTMENT OWNER' means the person owning an Apartment in the 'SOBHA JASMINE' and holding 10 shares of 'SOBHA JASMINE CONDOMINIUM'."*

**Clause 1.3** states:

> *"All present and future owners, tenants, future tenants or their employees, licensees or any other person who might use the facilities of the building in any manner, are subject to the regulation set forth in these Bye-laws."*

**Clause 10 — Vote to Be Cast in Person** states:

> *"Votes shall be cast in person, or by his/her/their duly constituted Power of Attorney Holder."*

**Clause 6 — Joint Apartment Owners** states:

> *"Where an Apartment has been purchased by two or more persons jointly, they shall be jointly entitled to the Apartment and the shares of the Association shall be issued in their joint names, but the person whose names stands first in the Share Certificate shall alone have the right to vote."*

**Summary:** All Apartment Owners (or their Power of Attorney holders) are eligible to attend. For joint owners, all may attend but only the first-named on the Share Certificate may vote. The Bye-Laws do not explicitly address whether tenants or other residents may attend as observers — **this is a gap**.

**Disqualification — Clause 7:**

> *"No Apartment Owner shall be entitled to vote on the question of the election of members of the Board of the President, Secretary, Treasurer, or any other Office Bearer, or be entitled to stand for election to such office, if he/she is in arrears on the last day of the year in respect of his/her contribution for common expenses to the Association for more than 60 days."*

**Note:** Clause 7 restricts voting and standing for election only. It does not explicitly bar attendance at the meeting. Also, this disqualification is specifically for election-related voting — **the Bye-Laws are silent on whether arrears disqualify an owner from voting on non-election matters**. This is a **gap**.

---

## 5. What Matters Can Be Discussed in the SGM?

**Clause 14** restricts the scope of discussion:

> *"No other business shall be transacted at a Special Meeting, except as stated in the notice, without the consent of four-fifths of the Owners present in person."*

This means the matters that can be discussed are:

1. **Any matter stated in the notice** — the agenda items for which the SGM was convened.
2. **Any other matter** — but only if four-fifths (80%) of the owners present in person consent.

The Bye-Laws do not enumerate specific categories of matters that can or cannot be taken up in an SGM. In principle, any business of the Association that the Board or a majority of owners considers necessary can be the subject of an SGM.

Certain matters are referenced elsewhere in the Bye-Laws that could logically be SGM agenda items:

- **Removal of Managers** (Clause 23): *"At any regular or special meeting, duly called, any one or more of the Managers may be removed, with or without cause, by a majority of the Apartment Owners."*
- **Amendment of Bye-Laws** (Clause 53): *"These Bye-laws may be amended by the Association in a duly constituted meeting for such purpose."*

---

## 6. What Constitutes a Valid SGM? (Quorum Requirements)

Two clauses govern quorum for meetings of the Association:

**Clause 9 — Quorum** states:

> *"Except as otherwise provided in these Bye-laws, the presence in person of a majority of owners shall constitute a Quorum."*

Per **Clause 2.6**, "majority" means owners holding **51% of the votes**.

**Clause 16 — Adjourned Meetings** provides a fallback:

> *"If the meeting of owners cannot be organised because a quorum has not attended, the owners who are present, may adjourn the meeting to a time not less than forty-eight hours from the time the original meeting was called. If at such adjourned meeting also, no quorum is present, the owners present in person, being not less than 20% shall form a quorum."*

**Summary:**

| Scenario | Quorum Required |
|---|---|
| First convening of SGM | Majority of owners (51% of votes) present in person |
| Adjourned meeting (first adjournment) | Majority of owners (51% of votes) |
| Adjourned meeting (second adjournment) | 20% of owners present in person |

**Important:** The 48-hour minimum gap between the original meeting and the adjourned meeting must be observed.

---

## 7. What Items Can Be Discussed or Voted On in the SGM?

This overlaps with Section 5, but the key distinction is between **discussion** and **voting**.

**Clause 14** governs what may be transacted:

> *"No other business shall be transacted at a Special Meeting, except as stated in the notice, without the consent of four-fifths of the Owners present in person."*

"Transacted" encompasses both discussion and voting. The items that can be voted on are those stated in the notice, plus any additional items approved by 80% of those present.

**Clause 8 — Voting** establishes the voting unit:

> *"Each Apartment Owner and share holder whether joint Owner or otherwise will have the right of one vote for each Apartment."*

**Clause 10** establishes how votes are cast:

> *"Votes shall be cast in person, or by his/her/their duly constituted Power of Attorney Holder."*

**Gap:** The Bye-Laws do not specify whether proxy voting (as distinct from Power of Attorney) is permitted. Only voting "in person" or by "duly constituted Power of Attorney Holder" is mentioned.

---

## 8. If an Item Is Not Mentioned in the SGM Agenda, Can It Be Discussed?

Yes, but with a very high threshold.

**Clause 14** states:

> *"No other business shall be transacted at a Special Meeting, except as stated in the notice, without the consent of four-fifths of the Owners present in person."*

This means:

- **Default rule:** Non-agenda items cannot be transacted.
- **Exception:** Non-agenda items may be transacted if **four-fifths (80%)** of the Owners present in person give their consent.

This is a deliberately high bar, designed to protect owners who may have chosen not to attend because the agenda did not include matters of interest to them.

---

## 9. What Is the Minimum Number of Votes to Get an Item Approved?

The Bye-Laws do not specify a single, universal voting threshold for all SGM resolutions. The applicable threshold depends on what is being voted on:

**General matters — Clause 2.6:**

> *"'MAJORITY OF OWNERS' means those owners holding 51% of the votes in accordance with the percentages assigned in the Declaration."*

For general business, a **simple majority** (51%) of those voting would typically be required, though the Bye-Laws do not explicitly state whether this is 51% of all owners or 51% of those present at a quorate meeting. **This is a gap.**

**Specific thresholds stated in the Bye-Laws:**

| Matter | Threshold | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Removal of a Manager | Majority of Apartment Owners | Clause 23 |
| Transacting non-agenda business at SGM | Four-fifths (80%) of Owners present in person | Clause 14 |
| Amendment of Bye-Laws | Owners representing at least 2/3rds of total apartments | Clause 53 |

**Clause 23 — Removal of Manager:**

> *"...any one or more of the Managers may be removed, with or without cause, by a majority of the Apartment Owners..."*

**Clause 53 — Amendment of Bye-Laws:**

> *"...no amendment shall take effect unless approved by owners representing at least 2/3rd of the total number of all Apartments in the Building as shown in the Declaration."*

**Gap:** For ordinary resolutions at an SGM (e.g., approving expenditure, policy changes), the Bye-Laws do not explicitly state the voting threshold. This should be clarified through a Bye-Law amendment or by reference to the Karnataka Apartment Ownership Act, 1972.

---

## 10. Is the Final Vote of the SGM Binding on the Community?

The Bye-Laws do not contain an explicit clause stating that SGM resolutions are binding. However, several provisions strongly imply this:

**Clause 11 — Powers and Duties of Association** states:

> *"The Association will have the responsibility of administrating the maintenance of the Building, common areas and the annual maintenance etc., approving the Annual Budget, establishing and collecting monthly assessments and arranging for the management of the Condominium in an efficient manner."*

**Clause 19 — Powers and Duties of Board** states:

> *"The Board shall have the powers and duties necessary for the administration of the affairs of the Association and may do all such acts and things as are not by law or by these Bye-laws directed to be exercised and done by the Owners."*

The Board's powers are subordinate to the Owners. Decisions taken by the Owners in a duly constituted meeting (including an SGM) direct the Board's actions. The Board cannot override a resolution passed by the Owners.

**Clause 1.4** states:

> *"The mere acquisition, or rental, or taking on licence of any of the dwelling Units... will signify that these Bye-laws are accepted, ratified and will be complied with."*

**Clause 51 — Compliance** states:

> *"These Bye-laws are set forth to comply with the requirements of the Karnataka Apartment Ownership Act, 1972. In case, any of these Bye-laws conflict with the provision of the said Act, it is hereby agreed and accepted that the provisions of the Act will apply."*

**Conclusion:** While the Bye-Laws do not explicitly use the word "binding," the structure of governance makes it clear that resolutions passed in a duly constituted SGM (with proper quorum and voting thresholds) are binding on all owners and the Board. The one exception is that the Karnataka Apartment Ownership Act, 1972 overrides any conflicting Bye-Law provision. **This is a partial gap — a statement of binding effect should ideally be added via Bye-Law amendment.**

---

## 11. Who and How Are SGM Minutes Supposed to Be Captured and Communicated?

**Clause 32 — Secretary** states:

> *"The Secretary shall keep the minutes of all meetings of the Board and the minutes of all meetings of the Association, he shall have charge of such books and papers as the Board may direct; and he shall, in general, perform all the duties incidental to the office of Secretary."*

This means:

- **Who captures minutes:** The Secretary.
- **Scope:** Minutes of *all meetings of the Association* — this includes SGMs.

**Clause 17 — Order of Business** specifies that meetings should include:

> *"(c) Reading of minutes of proceeding meeting"*

This confirms that minutes from previous meetings (including SGMs) are read at the next meeting.

**Gap:** The Bye-Laws do not specify:

- **How minutes are communicated** to owners who were not present at the meeting.
- **Timeline** within which minutes must be prepared or distributed.
- **Whether minutes must be approved** by attendees (though reading at the next meeting implies a ratification step).
- **Whether owners have a right to request copies** of the minutes outside of meetings.

This is a significant procedural gap that should be addressed.

---

## 12. Under What Circumstances Can Decisions Taken or Votes Taken in an SGM Be Nullified?

The Bye-Laws do not contain an explicit clause on nullification of SGM decisions. However, the following provisions are relevant:

**Procedural non-compliance** could be grounds for challenge:

- If the meeting was called without proper notice (Clause 15 — 7 to 15 days).
- If quorum was not met (Clause 9 — majority; or Clause 16 — 20% at adjourned meeting).
- If the matter voted on was not in the notice and did not receive 80% consent (Clause 14).

**Clause 53 — Amendment of Bye-Laws** implies that any prior SGM decision that effectively amends the Bye-Laws without a 2/3rds supermajority would be invalid:

> *"...no amendment shall take effect unless approved by owners representing at least 2/3rd of the total number of all Apartments in the Building as shown in the Declaration."*

**Clause 51 — Compliance** provides an overriding check:

> *"In case, any of these Bye-laws conflict with the provision of the said Act, it is hereby agreed and accepted that the provisions of the Act will apply."*

This means any SGM decision that conflicts with the Karnataka Apartment Ownership Act, 1972 would be void by operation of law.

**Gap:** The Bye-Laws are **silent** on:

- A formal process for challenging or nullifying SGM decisions.
- Whether a subsequent SGM can reverse a prior SGM's decision (and if so, what threshold applies).
- Appeal mechanisms or dispute resolution procedures.
- Time limits within which SGM decisions can be challenged.

This is a significant gap. The Karnataka Apartment Ownership Act, 1972 and general civil law would govern these matters in the absence of specific Bye-Law provisions.
